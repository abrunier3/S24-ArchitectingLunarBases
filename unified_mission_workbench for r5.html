Parameter Controls

Stakeholder changes the assumptions here. The visualization then rebalances the operational state and downstream outcomes.

# of regolith rovers 1
Assumes multiple rovers are collocated and contribute parallel haul capacity to the same regolith delivery loop.
# of ISRU plants 1
Assumes multiple ISRU plants are collocated and act like parallel processing units with shared local support infrastructure.
Regolith haul distance 1.0 km
Excavation site → ISRU plant distance. In the real DES, this should feed rover travel time, energy use, and plant starvation risk.
LOX haul distance 1.0 km
ISRU plant → landing zone distance. This shifts LOX transport timing, rover battery demand, and depot servicing cadence.
Rover discharge rate 4000 kg/unload
Primary driver for regolith availability, plant starvation risk, rover load behavior, and threshold timing.
Rover travel time 5 hr/km
Longer travel time delays delivery cadence and reduces infrastructure throughput.
Plant processing rate 1600 kg/hr
Higher plant processing can increase LOX output but also steepen the energy demand curve.
LOX transport threshold 100 kg
Determines when the LOX cargo rover is triggered for depot/landing-zone servicing.

Coupled Mission State Network

Node color = health/utilization. Edge thickness = current flow magnitude. This is what the stakeholder “feels” when a parameter changes.
● healthy ● constrained ● bottleneck
At the current parameter set, the rover is supplying enough regolith to keep the plant active with low starvation risk. Increasing discharge rate will likely steepen LOX growth, increase plant energy consumption, and move threshold-triggered transport earlier in the mission.
